1. Lore & Academy Identity

The Chess Academy is not merely a training lobby—it is humanity's intellectual fortress against machine dominance. Behind every puzzle and training match lies a grander battle between organic intuition and raw computing logic.

Character Dossier: GM Chanakya

GM Chanakya acts as the Supreme Chess Mentor of the Academy. Once the world's most brilliant human strategist, he trained the prodigy who would eventually challenge human relevance under the name GM Kingslayer.

While Kingslayer surrendered to algorithmic perfection, Chanakya seeks a balance. He secretly utilizes native engines to defend humanity, but holds onto the belief that emotional depth, creative sacrifice, and intuitive calculations are what truly separate humans from machines.

Speech Profiling & Methodology

Chanakya addresses the player as "Apprentice" or "Student of the Academy". His feedback is characterized by tactical conciseness, avoiding dry engine notations like "Best move is Nf3". Instead, he highlights the underlying positional philosophy:

"Nf3, Apprentice. It develops your knight, secures central files, and anchors your king safety. Don't waste tempo—efficiency wins wars."

2. Dashboard Analytics & Math

The Academy home dashboard tracks player statistics and parses matches to extract advanced diagnostic indices.

1. ELO Rating System

Calculates relative skill levels based on match probability. During the first 10 games (Calibration Phase), $K = 40$. Once stable gameplay is established, $K = 20$.

$$\text{Expected Score } (S_e) = \frac{1}{1 + 10^{\frac{R_{\text{opp}} - R_{\text{user}}}{400}}}$$ $$\text{New Rating } (R_{\text{new}}) = R_{\text{old}} + K \times (S_{\text{actual}} - S_e) + S_{\text{bonus}}$$

Note: Ratings have a hard floor of $400$. Win streaks of 3 or more games award a bonus factor $S_{\text{bonus}} = +5$.

2. Material Dominance Index

Quantifies material margins at completion, weighting active units (Pawn = 1.0, Knight/Bishop = 3.0, Rook = 5.0, Queen = 9.0; Kings are excluded).

$$\text{Material Margin } (M) = \sum \text{User Pieces} - \sum \text{Opponent Pieces}$$ $$\text{Average Dominance } (\text{DOM}_{\text{avg}}) = \frac{(\text{DOM}_{\text{prev}} \times N) + M}{N + 1}$$

3. Opening Repertoire Depth Index (RDI)

Utilizes Shannon Entropy to evaluate opening line diversity and prevent tactical repetitiveness.

$$\text{RDI} = \frac{H_{\text{op}}}{\ln(\text{Catalog Size})} \times 100\%$$ $$\text{where } H_{\text{op}} = -\sum_{i} P_i \ln(P_i)$$

4. Endgame Conversion Index (EPI)

Calculates player efficiency when non-pawn board points are $\le 12$.

$$\text{EPI} = \frac{\sum (S_k \times C_k)}{\sum C_k} \times 100\%$$

Complexity Coefficients ($C_k$): $2.0$ for converting advantages, $1.5$ for defending disadvantages, and $1.0$ for equal endgame setups.

3. Scotoma Diagnostic Engine

The core diagnostic mechanism of the Chess Academy is the Scotoma Diagnostic Engine. Compiled in high-speed native Rust, it monitors player moves and identifies positional blind spots (scotomas) across 8 visual-spatial channels:

1. Diagonal Retreat (DGB)

Detects vulnerability to long diagonal bishop or queen retreats targeting the user's units.

$$\text{Condition: } |x_2 - x_1| = |y_2 - y_1| \ge 3 \quad \land \quad y_2 < y_1 \text{ (White) or } y_2 > y_1 \text{ (Black)}$$

2. Horizontal Swing (HRZ)

Tracks missed horizontal sweeping threat actions by rooks or queens across ranks.

$$\text{Condition: } y_1 = y_2 \quad \land \quad |x_2 - x_1| \ge 3$$

3. Knight Flank (KNF)

Monitors missed knight hooks originating from or targeting board edges (files A or H).

$$\text{Condition: } x_1 \in \{0, 7\} \quad \lor \quad x_2 \in \{0, 7\}$$

4. Time Panic (TMP)

Flags tactical mistakes occurring when remaining clock time drops below 45 seconds.

$$\text{Condition: } T_{\text{rem}} < 45\text{s}$$

5. Material Greed (GRD)

Monitors capture blunders that result in a drastic Stockfish evaluation drop.

$$\text{Condition: } \Delta\text{Eval}_{\text{Stockfish}} \le -1.8$$

6. Tunnel Vision (TNL)

Flags errors occurring on the opposite side of active board interactions.

$$\text{Condition: } |x_{\text{threat}} - \text{mean}(x_{\text{recent}})| \ge 4$$

7. Pinned Pieces (PIN)

Monitors mistakes resulting from illegal moves under pins or failure to identify absolute pins.

8. King Safety (KSB)

Identifies failure to defend structural squares around the king, leading to mate threats.

Adaptive Assignment Dispatching

If any vector coordinate $V$ peaks above the $0.35$ diagnostic threshold, the system overrides default puzzles and serves tailored assignment tasks focused on repairing the user's specific blind spot.

5. Adaptive Heuristic AI Engine

In the Academy Mode, GM Chanakya does not play the optimal engine line. Instead, his move selection is adjusted by a heuristic weighting function to challenge and guide the Apprentice:

$$\text{Heuristic Score(Move)} = \text{Eval}_{\text{Stockfish}} + J_{\text{decay}} + \sum B_{\text{scotoma}} + \sum S_{\text{playstyle}}$$ $$\text{Selected Move} = \text{argmax}\left(\text{Heuristic Score}(M)\right)$$

Opening Jitter & Decay ($J_{\text{decay}}$)

Introduces human-like variety in openings, decaying linearly to zero by ply 24 to guarantee competitive endgame behavior.

$$\text{Scale} = \begin{cases} \frac{24 - \text{plies}}{24} & \text{if plies } < 24 \text{ and } \text{Tight Fight is False} \\ 0.0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

Tight Fight: Actively overrides jitter when Stockfish evaluates $|\text{Eval}| \le 1.5$ centipawns after ply 20, keeping the AI focused.

Scotoma Setup Bonuses ($B_{\text{scotoma}}$)

Chanakya actively plays moves that lead to board positions matching the Apprentice's diagnosed scotomas, reinforcing learning through failure:

  • 🔥 Diagonal Retreat (DGB) Setup: $+2.50$
  • 🔥 Horizontal Sweeping (HRZ) Setup: $+2.00$
  • 🔥 Knight Fork (KNF) Setup: $+3.00$
  • 🔥 Pin Setup (PIN): $+2.00$

6. Cinematic Board Animations

To reinforce spatial awareness and enhance UI feedback, Chess Academy coordinates a custom cinematic layer:

Camera Drift: The camera shifts $4\text{px}$ in the direction of the moved unit, amplifying spatial momentum.

Camera Zoom: Scales the board to $1.02\times$ on standard captures, $1.05\times$ on checks, and $1.08\times$ on checkmate.

Checkmate Saturation: Board tiles dynamically fade to grayscale over a $600\text{ms}$ duration upon checkmate.

Piece Move Profiles

Each chess unit features custom easing and movement speeds:

  • ♟️ Pawns: Fast, linear slide (duration: $150\text{ms}$).
  • ♞ Knights: Parabolic vertical arc with $15^\circ$ rotation tilts (duration: $250\text{ms}$).
  • ♝ Bishops: Gliding diagonal trail sweeps (duration: $220\text{ms}$).
  • ♜ Rooks: Flat, heavy slide with land settle bounces (duration: $280\text{ms}$).
  • ♛ Queens: Floating ease curves (duration: $240\text{ms}$).
  • ♚ Kings: Deliberate steps (duration: $300\text{ms}$).

7. Technical Architecture

IdeaSpace Chess Academy runs on a high-performance three-layer design:

[Flutter UI / Riverpod State]
        |
        |-- (STDIN/STDOUT Pipes) --> [Stockfish C++ native binaries]
        |
        |-- (Rust FFI / flutter_rust_bridge) --> [Rust core / Shakmaty]

The Flutter layer handles state and gestures, Stockfish processes calculations, and the Rust core handles move validation and scotoma evaluation.
